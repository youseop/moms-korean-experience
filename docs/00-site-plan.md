# Mom's Korean Experience — Site Plan

> Source of truth for the project's concept, structure, and content. Other planning docs in this folder build on this one.

## 1. Project Overview

A warm, mobile-first English-language website introducing three services offered by Youseop's mom in Jeongja-dong, Seongnam:

1. **Tours** — Local tours guided personally by Mom
2. **Cooking Class** — Home-style Korean cooking experience
3. **Stay** — A private room in Mom's home with daily home-cooked meals

**Target audience:** International travelers, especially Youseop's Korean language students, who have visited Korea at least once before and want a deeper, local experience beyond tourist spots.

**Brand feeling:** Warm, maternal, inviting. "Like visiting your Korean mom."

**Priority order:** Tours > Cooking Class > Stay (reflects expected demand — tours have the widest appeal, stay is the most committed experience)

## 2. Site Structure

### Home Page
A single scrolling page that introduces Mom and the three services in order of priority.

1. **Hero Section**
   - Warm photo of Mom (smiling, inviting)
   - Tagline: something like "Your Korean mom in Seoul"
   - Brief intro (1-2 sentences)
   - Primary CTA: "Explore Tours"

2. **Meet Mom** (short intro)
   - Mom's story in 2-3 paragraphs
   - Why she started this
   - Personal, warm tone

3. **Three Experiences Preview** (in priority order)
   - Card 1: Tours → link to Tours page
   - Card 2: Cooking Class → link to Cooking Class page
   - Card 3: Stay → link to Stay page
   - Each card: hero image + 1-line description + "Learn more"

4. **Gallery Preview** (mobile-optimized grid)
   - Mix of Mom, food, places, the home
   - Tap to expand

5. **Featured Reviews**
   - 2-3 highlighted reviews across all services

6. **Final CTA / Contact**
   - "Have a question? Get in touch"
   - Link to Tally inquiry form

### Tours Page (Priority 1)

1. **Hero** — Tour photo + headline: "See Korea through a local mom's eyes"
2. **The Concept**
   - Not a scripted tourist tour — Mom takes you to places she and her family actually love
   - Hidden local spots most tourists never find
   - Hard-to-reach places? Mom can drive you personally
   - For travelers who've already been to the famous spots
3. **Mom's Favorite Places**
   - Gallery + short descriptions of the kinds of places she loves
   - Mix of Seoul hidden gems + spots outside Seoul
   - Emphasize: real local, not touristy
4. **How It Works**
   - Step 1: Tell Mom what you're interested in
   - Step 2: She designs a custom day based on your interests
   - Step 3: Spend the day with your Korean mom
   - Emphasize the conversation-based customization
5. **Sample Itineraries** (optional — 2-3 example days)
   - Example: "A day in Seoul's quiet corners"
   - Example: "Outside the city — nature + local food"
6. **Reviews** (2 reviews already prepared with text/image/video)
7. **Inquiry CTA** → Tally form

### Cooking Class Page (Priority 2)

1. **Hero** — Photo of Mom cooking or finished dishes + headline
2. **The Experience**
   - Cook alongside Mom in her home kitchen
   - Eat what you make together as a meal
   - Relaxed, conversational, homey atmosphere
3. **Atmosphere Gallery** — Photos of the kitchen, ingredients, cooking moments, shared meals
4. **What You Can Cook** — Three set menus:
   - **Menu A:** Kimbap + Bulgogi + Seasonal Special
   - **Menu B:** Kimbap + Japchae + Seasonal Special
   - **Menu C:** Korean-style Chicken + Kimbap + Seasonal Special
   - Plus: "Want to cook something else? Tell us what you'd like to learn and we can plan it together."
   - Allergies and vegetarian/vegan options: fully accommodated with advance notice
5. **Reviews**
6. **Inquiry CTA** → Tally form

### Stay Page (Priority 3)

1. **Hero** — Photo of the room or the home + headline
2. **The Home & The Room**
   - Photos of the private room
   - Private bathroom right outside the room (exclusive to guests)
   - Photos of the home and surroundings
3. **Mom's Home Cooking, Every Day**
   - The highlight: a home-cooked dinner made with love, included every day
   - Photos of meals
4. **Why Jeongja?**
   - Youseop has lived in 5+ Korean cities including Seoul and Busan — Jeongja is the most livable
   - Tancheon stream right in front of the building
   - Beautiful parks along Tancheon
   - Quiet, residential, real local life
5. **Getting Around**
   - Jeongja Station: 5 minutes walking
   - Gangnam Station: 20 minutes by subway
   - Myeongdong: 40 minutes by bus
   - Best for travelers staying 1+ weeks who want a real neighborhood base
6. **Meet Maru** 🐶
   - Introduction to Maru, the 14-year-old Schnauzer
   - Photos of Maru
   - Warm, family-feel moment
7. **Reviews**
8. **Inquiry CTA** → Tally form

## 3. Design Direction

**Tone & Mood**
- Warm, maternal, inviting
- Like opening a photo album from a trip that felt like home

**Color Palette**
- Base: warm cream / soft beige
- Accent: warm terracotta or sage green (TBD during design)
- Text: soft dark brown (not harsh black)

**Typography**
- Headings: warm serif (feels like a handwritten letter)
- Body: clean, readable sans-serif
- Occasional handwritten/script accents for quotes or Mom's voice

**Imagery**
- Images are the heroes — Mom, food, places, the home, Maru
- Large, full-width photos on mobile
- Gallery grids with lazy loading
- Placeholder images for prototype; real photos to replace later

**Mobile-First**
- Designed for phones first
- Large touch targets
- Single-column flow
- Smooth scrolling with clear section breaks
